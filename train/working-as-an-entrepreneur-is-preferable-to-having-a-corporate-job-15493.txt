Discussion Title: Working as an entrepreneur is preferable to having a corporate job

1. Working as an entrepreneur is preferable to having a corporate job
1.1. Con: Many people are more suited for corporate work than entrepreneurial.
1.2. Pro: As an entrepreneur, the earning potential is greater than working for a corporation.
1.2.1. Con: The potential for loss is just as great as well.
1.2.1.1. Pro: There is too much [financial risk](https://www.entrepreneur.com/article/238319) involved with being an entrepreneur.
1.2.1.1.1. Pro: Eight out of 10 new businesses [fail](https://www.forbes.com/sites/ericwagner/2013/09/12/five-reasons-8-out-of-10-businesses-fail/#5aea2cb36978) within the first 18 months.
1.2.1.1.2. Con: There is equally great potential for financial success.
1.2.2. Pro: Entrepreneurs earn[7%](https://www.forbes.com/sites/steveblank/2018/04/10/why-entrepreneurs-start-companies-rather-than-join-them/#27279446fd26) more than they would have in a corporate job.
1.3. Pro: Owning a business means unlimited upside
1.4. Con: -> See 1.2.1.1.
1.5. Con: Owning a business means having full responsibility.
1.5.1. Con: This responsibility can be empowering and allow people to enjoy their job more rather than having to work under someone else's decisions.
1.6. Pro: Entrepreneurs can define their own hours of work.
1.6.1. Con: Entrepreneurs often struggle with work/life balance, many working more than [50 hours](https://www.forbes.com/sites/forbestechcouncil/2018/01/25/entrepreneurs-need-to-pay-more-attention-to-work-life-balance-heres-how/#318ea9c46b33) a week.
1.6.1.1. Pro: Work might be much more stressful.
1.6.1.1.1. Pro: Stress can cause [anxiety and depression.](https://www.webmd.com/depression/features/stress-depression#1)
1.6.1.2. Con: Although entrepreneurs may work more hours per week than employees, this larger amount of hours may feel more worthwhile than a lesser amount of hours worked as an employee of a business
1.7. Pro: Entrepreneurs can build something from the ground up that is truly their own.
1.7.1. Con: It's easier to work for a corporation that is already started, than from the ground up.
1.8. Con: A corporate job provides a stable pay check every month.
1.9. Con: Most startups go bankrupt.
1.9.1. Pro: [Eighty-two percent](https://www.fundera.com/blog/small-business-statistics) of startups fail because of cash-flow issues.
1.10. Con: When your business fails you have to fire your loyal employees.
1.11. Pro: Entrepreneurs have full decision-making capability for their business.
1.11.1. Con: Have full decision making capacity puts a lot of [stress](https://www.entrepreneur.com/article/251563) on the entrepreneur.
