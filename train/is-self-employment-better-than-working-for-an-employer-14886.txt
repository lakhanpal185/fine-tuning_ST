Discussion Title: Is self-employment better than working for an employer?

1. Self-employment is better than working for an employer.
1.1. Con: Need to maintain reputation
1.1.1. Con: One still needs to maintain personal reputation within the business when working for an employer.
1.2. Con: Have to be more competitive
1.2.1. Con: In order to get a job in the first place it is necessary to be be an extremely competitive candidate.
1.3. Pro: There is no threat of being fired.
1.3.1. Con: You can still go bankrupt.
1.4. Pro: Self-employment is likely to lead to higher job satisfaction.
1.4.1. Con: It might also lead to higher levels of stress and anxiety
1.4.2. Pro: It is easier to be more motivated and passionate as you are working towards building your own dream/wealth and not someone else's.
1.5. Pro: One may get a higher share of the earnings and more profit.
1.5.1. Con: It is not guaranteed that self-employment will lead to more profit.
1.5.2. Con: This is under the assumption that one can create enough business to bring in a salary, which is not always easy to do.
1.5.3. Pro: Some of the richest people in the world set up their own companies.
1.6. Pro: Self-employment allows for one to have greater work flexibility.
1.6.1. Pro: Self-employed people can set their own hours, meaning they are not bound by a demanding boss.
1.6.2. Pro: Self-employed people have the power to delegate tasks to employees, meaning they don't always have to put in extra hours themselves.
1.7. Pro: Self-employment contributes more to the society by creating job opportunities.
1.8. Pro: Self-employment allows more room to be more creative and innovative.
1.9. Con: There is a higher risk associated with self-employment.
1.9.1. Pro: Higher risks means it is more difficult to start a family.
1.9.2. Con: One doesn't risk being fired when one is their own boss.
1.9.2.1. Pro: Being an employee is not without risk either: if the company is not making profit, then because the employer can't reduce the employee's salary, his only option is to fire the employee.
1.9.3. Pro: There is a chance of losing one's own money when self-employment fails as opposed to not losing it when an employing business incurs losses.
1.10. Con: Owners often get burnout.
1.10.1. Pro: Many successful businessmen like to take all decisions themselves, as they have so much at stake. This puts additional pressure on them, which may lead to burnout.
1.10.2. Pro: Self-employment leads to a higher rate of stressful situations
1.10.2.1. Con: In regular jobs there are stressful situations too.
1.10.3. Con: -> See 1.6.1.
1.10.4. Con: -> See 1.6.2.
