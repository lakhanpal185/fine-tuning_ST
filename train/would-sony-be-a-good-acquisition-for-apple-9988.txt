Discussion Title: Would Sony be a good acquisition for Apple?

1. Sony would be a good acquisition for Apple.
1.1. Pro: Sony has a huge team of engineers.
1.1.1. Pro: Apple has dozens of engineering projects that would benefit from an infusion of talent.
1.1.2. Con: Adapting tens of thousands of employees to Apple’s culture would require an enormous amount of work.
1.1.2.1. Con: There is no reason why Apple would have to keep all of those people employed - they could just fire them.
1.2. Con: Apple is already too powerful
1.2.1. Pro: A proposed deal might not be approved by the regulatory bodies.
1.2.2. Pro: Proposing a deal that wouldn't be approved might seed doubts about other acquisitions that Apple attempted in the future.
1.3. Pro: Sony has a powerful film production business.
1.3.1. Con: This production team does not reflect the same standards of family-friendly content as Apple has.
1.3.2. Pro: It is one of the six biggest film studios. \[[en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sony_Pictures)\]
1.4. Con: Given Sony’s rapid rebound in market value, it may be overpriced.
1.4.1. Pro: Even if Sony is not overpriced, the portion of assets that could be leveraged in Apple’s business could be less than its market cap.
1.4.1.1. Con: Unusable assets could simply be sold off.
1.4.2. Con: A historic alliance like this would carry significant intangible value.
1.4.3. Pro: Sony’s market cap of 63 billion is twice what it was in 2016.
1.4.3.1. Con: That is a good thing.
1.5. Con: Sony uses Windows internally.
1.5.1. Con: The endeavor of understanding the needs of Windows users would be valuable for Apple executives.
1.5.1.1. Con: They can do this without purchasing the company.
1.5.2. Con: Apple has recently shown an interest in selling corporate systems.  Observing a massive corporation shift from Windows to macOS with an all-access pass would be invaluable.
1.5.3. Pro: Even if most workflows can be ported from Windows, the process can be frustrating, wasteful, slow, and convoluted.
1.5.4. Con: It would be fine to continue using Windows in situations where that was preferred.
1.6. Con: Sony's business overlaps too much with Beats.
1.6.1. Con: Companies have often been known to have multiple competing brands amongst their purchases without this presenting an issue.
1.6.1.1. Pro: [Unilever](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Unilever_brands#Ice_cream), for instance, owns Popsicle, Klondike, Talenti gelato, Breyers and Ben & Jerry's.
1.7. Con: Sony may still be compromised following the North Korean [hack](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sony_Pictures_hack).
1.7.1. Con: Apple could check Sony's security and apply its own standards to ensure there is no further risk.
1.8. Pro: Sony’s strong branding is a surprisingly good fit with Apple’s core strengths.
1.8.1. Pro: Sony’s brand stood for elegant design.
1.8.2. Pro: Sony’s brand stood for sturdiness.
1.8.3. Pro: Sony’s brand stood for ease of use.
1.8.3.1. Pro: This is similar to some of the strongest strains of Apple’s brand identity.
1.8.3.2. Con: Their product interfaces didn’t always live up to this ideal.
1.8.3.3. Con: Easy to use, but difficult to choose.
1.8.3.3.1. Pro: Sony products come in a bewildering number of variants.
1.8.3.3.2. Pro: If you can’t choose, you can’t use.
1.9. Pro: Japanese culture would fit with that of Apple.
1.9.1. Pro: Japan’s technology culture fits with Apple.
1.9.1.1. Pro: Premium devices are valued by Japanese consumers.
1.9.1.2. Con: Apple’s products are seen as rigid and not customizable.
1.9.2. Pro: Japan’s music culture could fit well with Apple Music.
1.9.2.1. Pro: Beats 1 broadcasts from London, New York, and Los Angeles.  Tokyo would be an ideal fourth geographic location.
1.9.2.1.1. Pro: Billions of people can travel to Tokyo far more easily than they could access the other studios.
1.9.2.1.1.1. Pro: These people represent demographics that are incredibly important to Apple.
1.9.2.1.1.1.1. Pro: Apple carries out a social empowerment campaign that consistently features people whose racial identity is unfamiliar to Americans.
1.9.2.1.1.1.1.1. Pro: Improved comprehension of cultures such as Vietnam or Bangladesh could yield musicians who helped Apple Music customers continue to broaden their exposure.
1.9.2.2. Pro: Apple Music has taken a “pop-first” approach to redefining our access to music.  Japan is known for powerful pop culture.
1.9.2.3. Con: The advanced level of development in Japan’s music institutions could make integration challenging.
1.9.3. Con: Japan’s business culture could be challenging for Apple to comprehend.
1.9.3.1. Con: It would be mutually beneficial for the two cultures to learn more about each other.
1.9.3.2. Con: Extreme deference to management could be a good fit for Apple’s process.
1.9.3.2.1. Con: A workforce willing to offer reality checks is fundamental in technology work.
1.9.3.2.2. Pro: Apple management understands technology flawlessly.
1.9.3.2.3. Pro: Agreeableness is connected to career success in Japan. \[[sciencedirect.com](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0889158318300054)\]
1.10. Pro: Sony is a post-PC business. \[[Sony sold its Vaio business in 2014](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Post-PC_era)\]
1.10.1. Pro: Sony made the best Windows machines on the mass market.  Apple taking them on would send a message of redemption, hope, forgiveness, and dominance.
1.10.2. Con: Its unlikely that any of Sony’s laptop technology would help Apple improve its operation.
1.11. Con: -> See 1.1.2.
1.12. Pro: Sony has a plethora of product lines.
1.12.1. Con: Apple tends to consolidate, keeping alternatives to a minimum.  It is likely that they would therefore choose to cancel the majority of Sony’s lines.
1.12.1.1. Pro: This strategy has worked before.
1.12.1.2. Con: This approach would cancel out some of the market value acquired.
1.12.1.3. Con: This would be frustrating for Sony employees.
1.12.1.4. Con: This would be disruptive for Sony’s org chart.
1.12.1.5. Pro: Some Sony employees would appreciate this.
1.12.2. Pro: Sony’s PlayStation business is strong.
1.12.2.1. Pro: PlayStation’s system interfaces exhibit room for improvement.  Apple might be able to improve them.
1.12.2.2. Con: The portability of PlayStation software to iOS gaming platforms is very limited.
1.12.2.2.1. Con: It can be good to have no overlap between platforms.
1.12.2.2.1.1. Pro: Reducing analysis paralysis is part of Apple’s strategy.
1.12.2.2.1.2. Pro: There are many market segments that are skeptical of iOS.  Selling them PlayStation content could tap an otherwise unavailable market.
1.12.2.2.1.3. Pro: Segmentation can help evaluate performance of strategies in independent environments.
1.12.2.2.1.4. Con: Customers would be confused if some of their saved game data could be accessed on either platform, but some could not.
1.12.3. Pro: Sony has a lineage of savvy, sexy audio equipment.
1.13. Pro: Sony has reams of fantastic camera technology.
1.13.1. Pro: Sony entered the market after digital camera became popular, and rapidly created new approaches to many problems. \[[bhphotovideo.com](https://www.bhphotovideo.com/explora/photography/features/sonys-innovative-journey-camera-design-and-technology)\]
1.13.2. Pro: Camera technology is hugely important to Apple's iPhone business.
1.14. Pro: Steve Jobs had an incredibly special respect for Sony.
1.14.1. Pro: Steve wanted Sony laptops to run OSX.  This is dramatically different from his stance towards other computer manufacturers. \[[appleinsider.com](https://appleinsider.com/articles/14/02/05/steve-jobs-wanted-sonys-vaio-computers-to-run-mac-os-x)\]
