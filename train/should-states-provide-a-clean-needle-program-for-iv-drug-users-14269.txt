Discussion Title: Should States Provide A Clean Needle Program for IV Drug Users?

1. States should provide a clean needle exchange program for IV drug users.
1.1. Con: Drug dealers sell drugs close to needle exchanges knowing they will meet many potential customers.
1.1.1. Con: It will be easier for police to track and catch drug dealers if they are concentrated around one area.
1.2. Pro: Having a clean needle exchange helps education people about addiction.
1.3. Pro: Having a clean needle exchange brings a voice to local addicts.
1.4. Pro: Long term plans to treat addiction will be more effective if accompanied by harm reduction in the short term.
1.5. Pro: If the state would have the taxpayers to pay for it. They can also make sure the needles are filled it with Potassium chloride
1.5.1. Con: Normal doses of potassium chloride are [fatal](https://doi.org/10.1016%2F0300-9432%2877%2990062-0) when given to people who are not hypokalaemic.
1.6. Pro: Having a Clean Needle Program, will help save lives.
1.6.1. Pro: Having a clean needle exchange puts life-saving Narcan in the hands of people that can actually use it to save a life.
1.6.1.1. Pro: [Naloxone \(Narcan\)](https://www.drugabuse.gov/publications/naloxone-opioid-overdose-life-saving-science/naloxone-opioid-overdose-life-saving-science) is able to restore normal breathing quickly to stop the life-threatening effects of overdosing.
1.7. Con: A clean needle exchange brings drug users to certain locations where they are not desired.
1.8. Con: A clean needle exchange helps people use drugs.
1.8.1. Con: While it may help people use drugs more safely, needle exchanges do not promote or advertise the use of drugs.
1.8.1.1. Con: Making the tools for using drugs more accessible, can be enticing simply because it is more convenient to continue than to quit.
1.8.1.1.1. Con: Finding a needle isn't that hard currently, and the difficulty is not enough to make people quit.
1.8.1.2. Pro: [In addition](https://www.verywellmind.com/what-is-a-needle-exchange-22056) to clean needles, the exchange programs also provides users with resources for recovery, health advice, and local groups.
1.8.2. Pro: Giving out clean needles supplies users with the tools necessary to continue using, which is counter-intuitive.
1.8.3. Con: Giving clean needles helps to prevent a deeper problem, such as medical care for viruses and illnesses contracted through dirty needle use.
1.8.4. Con: Needle exchanges are often done in discreet places, such as alleys, and are not likely to encourage new users.
1.8.5. Con: If you're at a place in life where you're going to inject the hard drugs that you need needles for, a clean or dirty needle is most likely not going to be the determining fact.
1.9. Con: No one is likely to want to pay for this.
1.9.1. Con: Needle programs reduce the costs of HIV treatment.
1.9.1.1. Con: HIV infections contracted through dangerous and risky behavior should not be a tax payer responsibility.
1.9.1.1.1. Con: Following this logic, all other types of self-inflicted health issues would also not be funded, for example bad lifestyle/diet, which accounts for [7/10 deaths worldwide.](https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/2016/10/06/seven-in-ten-deaths-fuelled-by-diet-and-lifestyle-factors-study/)
1.9.1.2. Pro: The government has a direct incentive to expend taxpayer money to reduce the spread of dangerous and debilitating infectious disease among its population.
1.9.2. Pro: Businesses close to needle exchanges will be economically affected.
1.9.3. Pro: Tax payers will likely not want to pay to help people use drugs.
1.9.4. Con: This can be easily paid for by private donations and fundraisers.
1.10. Pro: Having access to clean needles stops or slows down the spread of Hep C and HIV.
1.10.1. Pro: Sharing [used needles](http://harmreduction.org/issues/hepatitis-c/overview/hepatitis-c-transmission/) is one of the top ways to transmit Hepatitis C.
1.11. Con: Children will see this and be more likely to become IV users.
1.11.1. Con: Children see all sorts of people - drug users, homeless people, people having mental issues, abusive people, etc. This does not mean that they will imitate, or even become, any of the above.
