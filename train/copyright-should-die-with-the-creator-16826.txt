Discussion Title: Copyright should die with the creator

1. Copyright should die with the creator
1.1. Pro: Continuing to enforce copyright after the creator's death easily allows for their creation to be used in ways they did not explicitly agree to.
1.2. Con: In extreme circumstances, such an approach may create an incentive for murder.
1.2.1. Pro: Viewing a potential victim as carrier of copyright helps to dehumanize the victim. This dehumanization is a general [characteristic](http://sk.sagepub.com/reference/violentcrime/n211.xml) of motivation for murder.
1.3. Pro: "If artworks aren't protected by copyright they can be easier used to develope culture further.
1.4. Con: Copyright should only last for 20 years, no more and no less.
1.5. Pro: Copyright is immoral.
1.5.1. Pro: Art, music and information should be free and universally accessible, not private property.
1.6. Con: Creative work should be viewed in the same way as any other asset and it is up to the owner of that asset to determine who it will be made available to upon their death.
1.6.1. Pro: Creators have rights to their intellectual property that does and should include the right to pass down their property to immediate descendants and legal beneficiaries.
1.6.2. Pro: It would be unfair if this only applied to Copyright but not to other forms of wealth, like created companies.
1.7. Con: Such an approach would remove the financial incentive for people to create work later in life if they were hoping that their work would financially benefit those they cared about after they had passed away.
1.7.1. Pro: The creator may have a disabled child, for example, who would need to be cared for many years into the future. Such care will cost money.
1.8. Pro: Continuing to enforce copyright after the creator's death is a form of censorship that could limit the availability of new ideas.
1.8.1. Con: Because copyrights are only on designated items and procedures, their exclusion from public usage rather encourages new ideas because people cannot make free use of the copyrighted item.
1.8.2. Pro: A creators' family have no right to withhold creative works from society for their own financial gain.
1.8.3. Pro: It is wrong for companies to limit the availability of artistic works after the creator has already passed away purely for profit.
1.8.3.1. Con: Works created: by Employees, in a Joint Venture, as Contributions to Collective Works, from an Independent Contractor with a work product ownership clause, or for Specially Commissioned Work is the legal property of the Company, and not that of the individual, original, creator of the work. 1.8.3.1.1. Con: Statements of current law, as positive statements, are generally irrelevant to a normative discussion.
1.8.3.2. Pro: The main reason that it doesn't anymore is because companies like Disney wanted to have legal power over old creations. Copyright should only belong to the creator, and nobody else, therefore should die with them
1.8.3.2.1. Con: Circular Arbitrary Conjecture - The above is essentially "I don't believe it ever should have existed, so that's my reasoning for why it shouldn't exist"
1.8.3.3. Con: Companies make profit out of patents, creations and designs. The fact that the person or persons involved are no longer alive does not make the right of the company to make profit any less true.
1.8.3.3.1. Con: Companies could still make a profit from the material, regardless of whether they maintain exclusive ownership of it.
1.8.4. Pro: All creative works ultimately belong to society.
1.8.4.1. Con: By that logic copyright laws should not exist and then creators could not make any profits and thus sustain their creation activity.
1.8.4.2. Con: Art works are traded and thus private property.
1.8.4.2.1. Pro: [Salvator Mundi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_most_expensive_paintings) was bought for $450 million.
1.8.4.3. Pro: No work of creativity comes only from the individual. It is always a product of a range of environmental inputs as well, that couldn't occur without the existence of a society in which the individual lives.
1.8.5. Pro: Enforcing copyright laws limits educational opportunities.
1.8.5.1. Pro: Many great works are expensive, but become more easily purchasable without copyright protection.
1.8.6. Pro: Reducing the length of copyright would allow important information to enter the public domain more quickly.
