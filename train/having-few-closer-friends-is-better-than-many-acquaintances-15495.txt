Discussion Title: Having few closer friends is better than many acquaintances

1. Having few closer friends is better than many acquaintances
1.1. Pro: You can reach a deeper bond with fewer friends.
1.1.1. Pro: It takes [time](https://michaelsseaver.com/lifecoaching/building-relationships-takes-time/), trust, and intimacy to feel close to someone. Investing energy in fewer friends allows you to build closer relationships with them.
1.1.2. Pro: Close friendships can provide support in a way that casual friendships cannot.
1.1.2.1. Pro: Close friendships have a positive effect on mental health, as intimate friendships can provide a [shoulder](https://spunout.ie/opinion/article/friends-or-acquaintances) to cry on.
1.1.3. Pro: Many people feel more [listened to](https://www.theodysseyonline.com/large-friend-groups-vs-small-ones) and valued by a close friend, as opposed to feeling unacknowledged by a more distance acquaintance.
1.1.4. Con: We need more than intimate support from people around us. A larger friend group provides more people who can give [advice](http://www.youne.com/the-importance-of-having-friends/) and help when we need it.
1.1.5. Con: Forming a closer bond with less people leaves you more vulnerable if the friendship doesn't last.
1.1.5.1. Pro: Other [relationships](https://www.liveabout.com/why-most-friendships-dont-last-1384825) and changes in circumstances mean that most friendships do not last.
1.1.5.2. Pro: The time you put into a close friendship, and the openness and vulnerability needed to form one, mean that you become very attached to the person.
1.1.5.3. Con: The vulnerability that close friendships create shows how meaningful they are for people. This means that it is worth the risk.
1.2. Pro: You get the most out of your time
1.3. Con: You may isolate yourself from other people.
1.3.1. Con: Being able to truly rely on few people is better than having a larger circle of unreliable friends.
1.3.2. Pro: Social isolation is a growing [problem](https://www.huffingtonpost.com/lena-aburdene-derhally/the-growing-problem-of-so_b_9847990.html?guccounter=1) in our society. Even people who seemingly have a lot of friends feel lonely.
1.3.3. Pro: It is difficult to predict which friendships will endure the test of time, and which will fade away. Having fewer friends is a lot more likely to leave you isolated later if the relationships fade.
1.4. Con: Your circle of friends will not expand as much
1.5. Con: Spending significant amounts of time with very few people may interfere with your social codes.
1.5.1. Pro: Some people may have to socialize and cultivate friendships in order to do their jobs.
1.5.2. Con: For many, arbitrary social codes simply provide a restriction on their happiness and freedom. They would be far happier if they acted as they feel comfortable with fewer people.
1.6. Con: The bigger a group of friends the more different knowledge you will learn.
1.6.1. Pro: Spending time with a wider group of people gives an opportunity to learn about different approaches to life. What we don't at first understand we [learn](https://www.theodysseyonline.com/big-friend-group-relevance-in-college) by spending time together.
1.6.2. Pro: Friends are vital to provide [motivation](https://www.boldsky.com/insync/pulse/2014/why-friends-are-important-top-six-reasons.html) and inspiration. A wider friend group means a wider spread of experience and opinions. This means they can help with more situations you face in life.
1.6.3. Con: You can learn from everyone you work with, and interact with. A close nit, smaller group of friends can provide emotional support that a large circle of acquaintances cannot.
1.6.3.1. Pro: -> See 1.1.
1.6.4. Con: When you interact more distantly with a larger group of acquaintances, rather than getting really close with fewer friends, you limit how much you can understand them, and so how much you can learn.
1.7. Pro: You get to appreciate your time with and without your friends more when you have a smaller, close group.
1.7.1. Pro: Having fewer closer friends allows time for time alone and self reflection.
1.7.1.1. Pro: Time alone is important to allow your brain to [unwind](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/high-octane-women/201201/6-reasons-you-should-spend-more-time-alone) and to focus.
1.7.1.2. Pro: Over [50%](https://introvertdear.com/news/spending-time-alone-might-be-the-best-way-to-rest-according-to-science/) of people find time alone restful and relaxing.
1.7.1.3. Pro: A larger group of friends requires a huge commitment to remain in touch with all of them. It's easier to build a regular schedule with fewer people.
1.7.1.4. Con: This can also leave people feeling lonely.
1.7.1.4.1. Pro: -> See 1.3.
1.7.2. Con: Being closer to some friends makes it more difficult to take time apart form them if they need you. This makes personal space more difficult to find.
1.8. Pro: You can get a deeper understanding of what your time is in the sense of the differences between when you are with other people and when you are with a very close friend.
