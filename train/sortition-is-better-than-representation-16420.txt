Discussion Title: Sortition is Better than Representation

1. Sortition is Better than Representation
1.1. Con: Sortition may result in professional bureaucrats having too much power.
1.2. Con: Sortition allows for unqualified individuals to be elected to positions of responsibility.
1.2.1. Con: Career politicians act out of duty, vocation or interest, which are better than doing something just because you have been randomly selected to do it.
1.2.2. Pro: Politicians need to be trained, otherwise their policies would be dangerous and probably disastrous.
1.3. Con: Sortition can lead to really bad outcomes if the wrong people are randomly chosen.
1.3.1. Pro: The whole point of public policy and governance is acute acumen in a particular domain to direct society in some progressive direction. Sortition without a preliminary filtering of people possessing some characteristics to that effect, is worse than representation as there's neither the assent of a populace nor is there a particularly beneficial skill set that's brought to the table. This can result in unstable polity/government in that society.
1.4. Pro: Sortition decreases the amount of polls & interaction needed between a representative & the public.
1.4.1. Con: Reducing the amount of interaction between the public and officials is not necessarily a good thing in a democratic system.
1.5. Con: The people elected have no incentive to try to do anything, let alone doing "good".
1.6. Pro: Democratic
1.7. Pro: Sortition decreases the length of time in a feedback loop between the implementation & effects of a policy.
1.8. Pro: Sortition avoids the problems of gerrymandering found in both direct & representative democracy.
1.8.1. Con: Laws can be implemented to avoid [gerrymandering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gerrymandering#Spain).
1.8.1.1. Pro: While a problem in the US, there are plenty of countries that do not have that issue because the [system](https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/monkey-cage/wp/2017/08/07/the-supreme-court-will-soon-consider-gerrymandering-heres-how-changes-in-redistricting-could-reduce-it/?utm_term=.1169b8610e9a) works differently.
1.8.2. Con: Representation doesn't pull people out of their careers & force them into office.
1.8.2.1. Con: Sortition can be made a voluntary thing.
1.8.3. Con: Sortition can be made to be discriminatory.
1.8.4. Con: Representation increases the accountability of the representatives.
1.8.5. Con: Representation allows for career politicians.
1.9. Pro: Sortition virtually eliminates the need for dishonest career politicians.
1.9.1. Con: It also eliminates the experience, know-how and willingness of honest politicians.
1.9.2. Con: Career politicians are better politicians because they get trained in politics.
1.9.2.1. Pro: Career politicians are better prepared since they need to [battle](http://www.bbc.com/news/uk-politics-23437111) other candidates.
1.9.2.1.1. Pro: They may need also need to go through assessments processes and be approved by other members of their party, when they are deemed ready and experienced enough.
1.9.2.1.1.1. Pro: In the UK, for example, Liberal Democrats and Conservatives go through [assessment processes](https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-politics-23437111) that are  difficult as well as very professionalized and based on competency.
