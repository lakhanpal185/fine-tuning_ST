Discussion Title: Should there be an international standard to ensure mental integrity?

1. There should be an international standard to ensure that technologies do not violate the mental integrity of individuals.
1.1. Con: How would you measure, police, and enforce? Who would then monitor the monitors?
1.2. Con: Countries should have the ability to determine the rights of their citizens.
1.3. Con: In order to protect society, some classes of criminals should not have the right to mental integrity.
1.4. Pro: Mental integrity is a fundamental human right.
1.4.1. Pro: Mental integrity is already [listed](http://fra.europa.eu/en/charterpedia/article/3-right-integrity-person) in the EU Charter of Fundamental Rights.
1.4.2. Con: There exists no universal definition of mental integrity.
1.5. Con: Treating certain medical conditions might take precedence over mental integrity.
1.6. Pro: Standards should be the same across the world because remote electronic technologies can cross physical national borders.
1.7. Con: It is not unethical to violate the mental integrity of individuals.
1.7.1. Con: A violation of mental integrity is an affront to the dignity of the individual and places that person at the whim of their controller.
1.8. Con: International standards will be ignored by individual countries.
1.8.1. Pro: Most major powers regularly ignore international [laws](https://qz.com/729524/chinas-activities-in-the-south-china-sea-are-illegal-and-destroying-the-environment-an-international-court-finds/), [norms](http://theconversation.com/russia-doesnt-just-violate-international-law-it-follows-and-shapes-it-too-92700), and [standards](https://www.vox.com/the-big-idea/2017/4/19/15345686/syria-un-strike-illegal-un-humanitarian-law).
1.9. Pro: Establishing international standards will encourage countries to take this issue more seriously.
